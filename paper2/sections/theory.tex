\section{Coverage guarantees}\label{sec:theory}

The guarantees in this section are deterministic statements about the
calibration algorithm's realized error sequence, in the adversarial style of
\citet{gibbs2021adaptive}: they hold for \emph{every} sequence of
nonconformity scores, with no exchangeability, stationarity, or independence
assumption; in particular, arbitrary serial dependence and arbitrary
cross-sectional dependence among stocks are covered. Dependence affects how
the deterministic frequency statements below relate to population
conditional probabilities (Remark~\ref{rem:dependence}), not the statements
themselves.

\subsection{Setup and notation}

On day $t$ the panel contains stocks $i \in \mathcal{I}_t$ with
$n_t = |\mathcal{I}_t|$, and the calibrator observes a membership vector
$\pi_t = (\pi_t(1), \dots, \pi_t(K))$ with $\pi_t(k) \ge 0$,
$\sum_k \pi_t(k) = 1$, produced by a regime estimator using only information
available at the forecast origin ($\mathcal{F}_{t-1}$-measurable). Each
stock-day carries a standardized nonconformity score $s_{i,t}$; for the
two-sided interval the upper side uses $s_{i,t}$ and the lower side uses
$-s_{i,t}$, each tracked independently, so we present one side. Write
$a \in (0,1)$ for the side's miss budget ($a = \alpha/2$ two-sided,
$a = \alpha$ for the one-sided risk head).

The calibrator maintains per-regime thresholds $q_t(k)$ and issues to every
stock the mixture threshold
\begin{equation}\label{eq:issued}
  \bar q_t \;=\; \sum_{k=1}^{K} \pi_t(k)\, q_t(k),
\end{equation}
(per-stock offsets $\delta_i$ are treated in
Remark~\ref{rem:offsets}). After the day's outcomes are observed, define the
miss indicators and the day's summed excess miss
\begin{equation}\label{eq:err}
  \mathrm{err}_{i,t} \;=\; \mathbf{1}\{ s_{i,t} > \bar q_t \},
  \qquad
  g_t \;=\; \sum_{i \in \mathcal{I}_t} \bigl( \mathrm{err}_{i,t} - a \bigr),
\end{equation}
and update every regime's threshold by one per-observation gradient step
gated by its membership:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:update}
  q_{t+1}(k) \;=\; q_t(k) \;+\; \eta_k\, \pi_t(k)\, g_t .
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:update} is online (sub)gradient descent on the pinball
loss of the \emph{issued} threshold, with the step applied per stock-day:
pooling means a regime's threshold advances by the number of stock-days it
governs, which is the design's point: on a 100-name panel a stress regime
accrues roughly $100\times$ the updates any single stock could provide.

We make one boundedness assumption, standard in this literature and
verifiable in-sample.

\begin{assumption}\label{ass:bounded}
Scores are bounded: $|s_{i,t}| \le B$ for all $(i,t)$.
\end{assumption}

Under Assumption~\ref{ass:bounded} the iterates \eqref{eq:update} remain in
the interval $[-B - \eta_k \bar n,\; B + \eta_k \bar n]$ with
$\bar n = \max_t n_t$: whenever $q_t(k) > B$ every score is covered, each
$g_t \le 0$ term pushes the threshold down, and a single step moves it by at
most $\eta_k n_t$; symmetrically from below.\footnote{MAD-standardized
log-volatility errors are empirically bounded ($|s| < 12$ across all 519{,}843
stock-days); alternatively the update can be clipped to $[-B, B]$ without
affecting any result below.}

\subsection{Per-regime coverage}

\begin{proposition}[Hard regimes]\label{prop:hard}
Let memberships be hard, $\pi_t \in \{0,1\}^K$, and let
$\mathcal{T}_k = \{ t \le T : \pi_t(k) = 1 \}$ with
$N_k = \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} n_t$ stock-days. Then for every regime $k$
and every score sequence,
\[
  \Bigl|\; \frac{1}{N_k} \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} \sum_{i \in \mathcal{I}_t}
     \bigl( \mathrm{err}_{i,t} - a \bigr) \;\Bigr|
  \;\le\; \frac{ 2B + \eta_k \bar n }{ \eta_k \, N_k }.
\]
Thus the realized miss frequency inside regime $k$ converges to the nominal
budget $a$ at rate $O(1/N_k)$ in the regime's \emph{stock-day} count, that
is, the pooled clock rather than the single-stock clock.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
With hard memberships, only regime $k$'s threshold moves on days
$t \in \mathcal{T}_k$, by exactly $\eta_k g_t$. Telescoping
\eqref{eq:update} over $\mathcal{T}_k$,
\[
  q_{T+1}(k) - q_{1}(k) \;=\; \eta_k \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} g_t
  \;=\; \eta_k \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} \sum_{i}
        \bigl( \mathrm{err}_{i,t} - a \bigr).
\]
Both iterates lie in $[-B - \eta_k \bar n, B + \eta_k \bar n]$, so the
left side is bounded by $2B + 2\eta_k \bar n$ in absolute value; dividing by
$\eta_k N_k$ gives the claim (absorbing one $\eta_k \bar n$ term, since
$q_1(k) \in [-B, B]$ by initialization from warmup quantiles).
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Soft regimes]\label{prop:soft}
For arbitrary soft memberships and every regime $k$, define the
$\pi$-weighted stock-day mass $W_k = \sum_{t \le T} \pi_t(k)\, n_t$. Then
\[
  \Bigl|\; \frac{1}{W_k} \sum_{t \le T} \pi_t(k) \sum_{i \in \mathcal{I}_t}
     \bigl( \mathrm{err}_{i,t} - a \bigr) \;\Bigr|
  \;\le\; \frac{ 2B + \eta_k \bar n }{ \eta_k \, W_k },
\]
i.e.\ coverage is valid at the nominal level in every regime's
\emph{membership-weighted} time, at rate $O(1/W_k)$. Moreover, summing the
telescoped identities over $k$ with weights $\eta_k^{-1}$ and using
$\sum_k \pi_t(k) = 1$ yields marginal validity:
\[
  \Bigl|\; \frac{1}{N} \sum_{t \le T} \sum_{i}
     \bigl( \mathrm{err}_{i,t} - a \bigr) \;\Bigr|
  \;\le\; \frac{ \sum_k \eta_k^{-1} \bigl( 2B + \eta_k \bar n \bigr) }{ N },
  \qquad N = \sum_t n_t .
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Telescoping \eqref{eq:update} for a fixed $k$ over all $t \le T$ gives
$q_{T+1}(k) - q_1(k) = \eta_k \sum_t \pi_t(k)\, g_t$; the first display
follows from the same boundedness as in Proposition~\ref{prop:hard}. For the
second display, divide each telescoped identity by $\eta_k$ and sum over
$k$:
$\sum_k \eta_k^{-1} \bigl( q_{T+1}(k) - q_1(k) \bigr)
 = \sum_t g_t \sum_k \pi_t(k) = \sum_t g_t$,
and bound the left side termwise.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[One-sided risk head]\label{prop:onesided}
The one-sided calibrator tracks a single upper threshold with $a = \alpha$
and the same update \eqref{eq:update}; Propositions~\ref{prop:hard}
and~\ref{prop:soft} apply verbatim, giving per-regime control of the
exceedance frequency
$\smash{ \frac{1}{W_k} \sum_t \pi_t(k) \sum_i \mathbf{1}\{ s_{i,t} > \bar q_t \} }
 \to \alpha$
at rate $O(1/W_k)$. Mapped through the location--scale VaR construction,
this is a regime-conditional breach-frequency guarantee.
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Remarks on scope}

\begin{remark}[Arbitrary dependence]\label{rem:dependence}
Propositions~\ref{prop:hard}--\ref{prop:onesided} are identities about
realized frequencies and hold pathwise: correlated stocks, volatility
clustering, and regime persistence do not weaken them. What dependence
\emph{does} govern is the effective information content of $N_k$ and $W_k$:
on a day when the cross-section moves as one, $n_t$ stock-days
contribute one effective observation, so the practical convergence clock
lies between calendar days in regime $k$ and stock-days in regime $k$. Our
empirical evaluation therefore reports conditional coverage directly (and
its uncertainty via date-clustered standard errors) rather than relying on
the worst-case rates above.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Estimated regimes]\label{rem:estimated}
The guarantees condition on the calibrator's own membership sequence
$\pi_t$ (the operationally meaningful object, since $\pi_t$ is what a
risk desk can compute at the forecast origin), not on a latent true
regime $\pi_t^\ast$. Coverage conditional on the truth differs from
coverage conditional on the estimate by at most the membership
misalignment: for hard regimes, the miss-frequency gap in regime $k$ is
bounded by the relative frequency of misclassified stock-days,
$\smash{ W_k^{-1} \sum_t | \pi_t(k) - \pi_t^\ast(k) |\, n_t }$. We quantify
this empirically rather than only in the worst case: replacing our filtered
memberships with full-sample smoothed (oracle) memberships changes stress
coverage by $+0.3$ percentage points (Section~\ref{sec:oracle}), so regime
estimation error is not the binding constraint in this application.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Per-stock offsets]\label{rem:offsets}
The implementation adds a small per-stock offset $\delta_i$ (an L2-shrunk
idiosyncrasy absorber) to the issued threshold. All propositions apply to
the pooled component with $\mathrm{err}_{i,t}$ as issued (offsets included
in the indicator), because the update \eqref{eq:update} uses exactly those
indicators; the ablation in Section~\ref{sec:ablations} shows offsets are
empirically negligible ($\Delta$ stress coverage $< 0.1$pp).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Adaptive rates]\label{rem:adaptive}
The zero-tuning configuration replaces each fixed $\eta_k$ with a
DtACI-style expert bank \citep{gibbs2022conformal} whose issued threshold is
a weight-average of expert thresholds plus a per-regime corrector $c_k$
updated by the \emph{same} issued-interval rule \eqref{eq:update} at a
universal rate $\eta_{\mathrm{corr}}$. Because expert thresholds and weights
are bounded, the corrector's telescoped identity reproduces
Proposition~\ref{prop:soft} for the issued interval with
$\eta_k = \eta_{\mathrm{corr}}$ and $B$ enlarged by the expert-bank span:
the aggregator buys adaptivity of the \emph{rate}, while the corrector
anchors realized coverage of what is actually issued. With a single-element
grid and $\eta_{\mathrm{corr}} = 0$ the adaptive path reduces exactly to
\eqref{eq:update} (regression-tested to $10^{-12}$).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[What is not claimed]\label{rem:notclaimed}
The propositions control realized miss \emph{frequencies} in
regime-weighted time over the evaluation horizon. They are not
finite-sample conditional-probability statements for an individual day
(impossible without distributional assumptions,
\citealp{foygelbarber2021limits}), and they do not by themselves guarantee
coverage inside sub-regime events shorter than the convergence scale;
the empirically binding case is the first days of a volatility spike
(Section~\ref{sec:onset}), where the score distribution shifts faster than
any tracker with bounded step size can follow.
\end{remark}
