\section{Coverage guarantees}\label{sec:theory}

The guarantees in this section are deterministic statements about the
calibration algorithm's realized error sequence, in the adversarial style of
\citet{gibbs2021adaptive}: they hold for \emph{every} sequence of
nonconformity scores, with no exchangeability, stationarity, or independence
assumption; in particular, arbitrary serial dependence and arbitrary
cross-sectional dependence among stocks are covered. Dependence affects how
these realized-frequency statements relate to population conditional
probabilities (Remark~\ref{rem:dependence}), not the statements themselves.
They are stated for \emph{hard} regime memberships, which is the
configuration used in every headline result (the $K = 4$ VIX-percentile
bins are one-hot), and for the implementation exactly as run: no per-stock
offsets, with the adaptive-rate configuration covered by
Proposition~\ref{prop:adaptive}. The soft-membership variant is discussed
in Section~\ref{sec:soft-theory}.

\subsection{Setup and notation}

On day $t$ the panel contains stocks $i \in \mathcal{I}_t$ with
$n_t = |\mathcal{I}_t|$, and the calibrator observes a membership vector
$\pi_t = (\pi_t(1), \dots, \pi_t(K))$ with $\pi_t(k) \ge 0$,
$\sum_k \pi_t(k) = 1$, produced by a regime estimator using only
information available at the forecast origin. Throughout
Sections~\ref{sec:hard-theory}--\ref{sec:adaptive-theory} memberships are
hard: $\pi_t \in \{0, 1\}^K$, and $\mathcal{T}_k = \{ t \le T : \pi_t(k) =
1 \}$ collects regime $k$'s days, with stock-day count
$N_k = \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} n_t$. Each stock-day carries a
standardized nonconformity score $s_{i,t}$; the two-sided interval tracks
the upper side on $s_{i,t}$ and the lower side on $-s_{i,t}$
independently, so we present one side. Write $a \in (0,1)$ for the side's
miss budget ($a = \alpha/2$ two-sided, $a = \alpha$ for the one-sided risk
head).

The calibrator maintains per-regime thresholds $q_t(k)$ and issues to every
stock the threshold of the day's active regime,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:issued}
  \bar q_t \;=\; \sum_{k=1}^{K} \pi_t(k)\, q_t(k)
  \;=\; q_t(k_t), \qquad k_t = \text{the regime with } \pi_t(k_t) = 1 .
\end{equation}
After the day's outcomes are observed, define the miss indicators and the
day's summed excess miss
\begin{equation}\label{eq:err}
  \mathrm{err}_{i,t} \;=\; \mathbf{1}\{ s_{i,t} > \bar q_t \},
  \qquad
  g_t \;=\; \sum_{i \in \mathcal{I}_t} \bigl( \mathrm{err}_{i,t} - a \bigr),
\end{equation}
and update every regime's threshold by one per-observation gradient step
gated by its membership:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:update}
  q_{t+1}(k) \;=\; q_t(k) \;+\; \eta_k\, \pi_t(k)\, g_t .
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:update} is online (sub)gradient descent on the pinball
loss of the \emph{issued} threshold, with the step applied per stock-day:
pooling means a regime's threshold advances by the number of stock-days it
governs. On a 100-name panel a stress regime therefore accrues roughly
$100\times$ the update steps any single stock could provide, though the
informational content of those steps depends on cross-sectional dependence
(Remark~\ref{rem:dependence}).

We make one boundedness assumption, standard in this literature and
verifiable in-sample.

\begin{assumption}\label{ass:bounded}
Scores are bounded: $|s_{i,t}| \le B$ for all $(i,t)$.\footnote{
MAD-standardized log-volatility errors are empirically bounded ($|s| < 12$
across all 519{,}843 stock-days).}
\end{assumption}

Under hard memberships the iterates \eqref{eq:update} remain in
$[-B - \eta_k \bar n,\; B + \eta_k \bar n]$ with $\bar n = \max_t n_t$: on
regime $k$'s days the issued threshold \emph{is} $q_t(k)$, so whenever
$q_t(k) > B$ every score is covered, $g_t \le 0$, and the threshold moves
down; a single step moves it by at most $\eta_k n_t$; symmetrically from
below. We note for later use that this self-correction argument is
specific to hard memberships, because it requires the issued threshold to
coincide with the component being updated
(Section~\ref{sec:soft-theory}).

\subsection{Per-regime coverage, fixed rates}\label{sec:hard-theory}

\begin{proposition}[Hard regimes]\label{prop:hard}
Let memberships be hard. Then for every regime $k$ and every score
sequence,
\[
  \Bigl|\; \frac{1}{N_k} \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} \sum_{i \in \mathcal{I}_t}
     \bigl( \mathrm{err}_{i,t} - a \bigr) \;\Bigr|
  \;\le\; \frac{ 2B + \eta_k \bar n }{ \eta_k \, N_k }.
\]
Thus the realized miss frequency inside regime $k$ converges to the nominal
budget $a$ at rate $O(1/N_k)$ in the regime's \emph{stock-day} count, that
is, the pooled clock rather than the single-stock clock. Summing the $K$
per-regime identities also bounds the marginal miss frequency:
$\bigl| N^{-1} \sum_{t \le T} \sum_i (\mathrm{err}_{i,t} - a) \bigr| \le
N^{-1} \sum_k \eta_k^{-1} (2B + \eta_k \bar n)$ with $N = \sum_t n_t$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
With hard memberships, only regime $k$'s threshold moves on days
$t \in \mathcal{T}_k$, by exactly $\eta_k g_t$. Telescoping
\eqref{eq:update} over $\mathcal{T}_k$,
\[
  q_{T+1}(k) - q_{1}(k) \;=\; \eta_k \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} g_t
  \;=\; \eta_k \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} \sum_{i}
        \bigl( \mathrm{err}_{i,t} - a \bigr).
\]
Both iterates lie in $[-B - \eta_k \bar n, B + \eta_k \bar n]$ by the
self-correction argument above, and $q_1(k) \in [-B, B]$ by initialization
from warmup quantiles, so the left side is bounded by
$2B + \eta_k \bar n$ in absolute value; dividing by $\eta_k N_k$ gives the
per-regime claim. The marginal claim follows by summing the telescoped
identities over $k$ (each day belongs to exactly one regime).
\end{proof}

\subsection{The adaptive configuration}\label{sec:adaptive-theory}

The headline method replaces each fixed rate $\eta_k$ with a per-regime
bank of $E$ expert thresholds $Q_t(k, j)$, one per rate $\eta_j$ in a
fixed dyadic grid with maximal element $\eta_{\max}$, combined by
exponential weights $w_t(k, \cdot)$ on pinball loss, plus a per-regime
additive corrector $c_t(k)$ updated at a universal rate
$\eta_{\mathrm{corr}}$. Each expert updates by rule \eqref{eq:update} with
its own rate and its \emph{own} miss indicators
$\mathbf{1}\{ s_{i,t} > Q_t(k, j) \}$; the issued threshold on a regime-$k$
day is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:adaptive-issued}
  \bar q_t \;=\; \textstyle\sum_j w_t(k, j)\, Q_t(k, j) \;+\; c_t(k),
\end{equation}
and the corrector updates with the \emph{issued} miss indicators
\eqref{eq:err}: $c_{t+1}(k) = c_t(k) + \eta_{\mathrm{corr}}\, \pi_t(k)\,
g_t$, from $c_1(k) = 0$. The coverage guarantee transfers to this
configuration; the bank buys adaptivity of the rate, while the corrector
anchors realized coverage of what is actually issued.

\begin{proposition}[Adaptive rates, hard regimes]\label{prop:adaptive}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:bounded} and hard memberships, the adaptive
configuration satisfies, for every regime $k$ and every score sequence,
\[
  \Bigl|\; \frac{1}{N_k} \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} \sum_{i \in \mathcal{I}_t}
     \bigl( \mathrm{err}_{i,t} - a \bigr) \;\Bigr|
  \;\le\; \frac{ 2B + (\eta_{\max} + \eta_{\mathrm{corr}})\, \bar n }
               { \eta_{\mathrm{corr}} \, N_k }.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each expert $(k, j)$ evolves only on regime-$k$ days, by rule
\eqref{eq:update} at rate $\eta_j$ with its own miss indicators; the
self-correction argument therefore applies expert-wise and gives
$|Q_t(k, j)| \le B + \eta_j \bar n \le B + \eta_{\max} \bar n =: C_w$
for all $t$. The weight-average in \eqref{eq:adaptive-issued} is a convex
combination, so it also lies in $[-C_w, C_w]$. For the corrector: on a
regime-$k$ day, if $c_t(k) > B + C_w$ then
$\bar q_t \ge c_t(k) - C_w > B$, every score is covered, and
$g_t \le 0$ pushes $c_t(k)$ down; a step moves it by at most
$\eta_{\mathrm{corr}} \bar n$; symmetrically from below. Hence
$|c_t(k)| \le B + C_w + \eta_{\mathrm{corr}} \bar n$ for all $t$.
Telescoping the corrector recursion over $\mathcal{T}_k$ from
$c_1(k) = 0$,
\[
  c_{T+1}(k) \;=\; \eta_{\mathrm{corr}} \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} g_t ,
\]
and bounding $|c_{T+1}(k)| \le B + C_w + \eta_{\mathrm{corr}} \bar n =
2B + (\eta_{\max} + \eta_{\mathrm{corr}}) \bar n$ gives the claim after
dividing by $\eta_{\mathrm{corr}} N_k$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[One-sided risk head]\label{prop:onesided}
The one-sided calibrator tracks a single upper threshold with $a = \alpha$
and the same updates; Propositions~\ref{prop:hard}
and~\ref{prop:adaptive} apply verbatim, giving per-regime control of the
realized exceedance frequency
$\smash{ N_k^{-1} \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} \sum_i
\mathbf{1}\{ s_{i,t} > \bar q_t \} } \to \alpha$ at rate $O(1/N_k)$.
Mapped through the location--scale VaR construction, this is a
regime-conditional breach-frequency guarantee at the panel level.
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Soft memberships: identity without an unconditional
bound}\label{sec:soft-theory}

The soft-membership variant (filtered HMM probabilities) uses the mixture
threshold $\bar q_t = \sum_k \pi_t(k) q_t(k)$ with the same update
\eqref{eq:update}. Telescoping still holds exactly for each $k$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:soft-identity}
  \sum_{t \le T} \pi_t(k)\, g_t
  \;=\; \frac{ q_{T+1}(k) - q_1(k) }{ \eta_k },
\end{equation}
so membership-weighted coverage error is controlled \emph{whenever the
iterates remain bounded}. The self-correction argument, however, does not
extend: with a mixture, $q_t(k) > B$ no longer implies the issued
threshold covers all scores (another component may be low), so misses can
continue to push an individual component upward. We therefore make no
unconditional coverage claim for soft memberships. What can be said is
conditional and ex-post verifiable: if $\max_{t \le T} |q_t(k)| \le C$ on
the realized path, then \eqref{eq:soft-identity} bounds the
$\pi$-weighted miss frequency by $2C / (\eta_k W_k)$ with
$W_k = \sum_t \pi_t(k) n_t$. In all our soft-HMM runs the realized
iterates remain in the same range as the hard-regime runs, and the
empirical conclusions are unchanged (Section~\ref{sec:oracle}); we treat
the soft variant as an empirical robustness exercise, not as a
configuration carrying the formal guarantee.

\subsection{Remarks on scope}

\begin{remark}[Arbitrary dependence]\label{rem:dependence}
Propositions~\ref{prop:hard}--\ref{prop:onesided} are identities about
realized frequencies and hold pathwise: correlated stocks, volatility
clustering, and regime persistence do not weaken them. What dependence
\emph{does} govern is the effective information content of $N_k$: on a day
when the cross-section moves as one, $n_t$ stock-days contribute one
effective observation, so the practical convergence clock lies between
calendar days in regime $k$ and stock-days in regime $k$, and the bound's
$\bar n$ term means a larger panel does not buy a clean $n$-fold gain. Our
empirical evaluation therefore reports conditional coverage directly, with
uncertainty from date-clustered Newey--West standard errors
(Section~\ref{sec:main}), rather than relying on the worst-case rates
above.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Panel-level, not stock-level]\label{rem:panel}
The propositions control miss frequencies aggregated across the panel
within each regime. They do not by themselves guarantee coverage for each
individual stock, or for each stock--regime pair: a pooled threshold can
over-cover some names and under-cover others around a valid regime
average. This is a real limitation of pooling, and we report the
cross-sectional distribution of realized per-stock coverage alongside the
aggregate tables so the reader can see the dispersion directly.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Estimated regimes]\label{rem:estimated}
The guarantees condition on the calibrator's own membership sequence
$\pi_t$ (the operationally meaningful object, since $\pi_t$ is what a
risk desk can compute at the forecast origin), not on a latent true
regime $\pi_t^\ast$. Coverage conditional on the truth differs from
coverage conditional on the estimate by at most the membership
misalignment: for hard regimes, the miss-frequency gap in regime $k$ is
bounded by the relative frequency of misclassified stock-days,
$\smash{ N_k^{-1} \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_k} | \pi_t(k) - \pi_t^\ast(k)
|\, n_t }$. Section~\ref{sec:oracle} probes this empirically with a
noncausal hindsight benchmark: substituting full-sample smoothed HMM
memberships for the causal filtered ones changes stress coverage by $+0.3$
percentage points, so regime estimation error does not appear to be the
binding constraint in this application.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[What is not claimed]\label{rem:notclaimed}
The propositions control realized miss \emph{frequencies} in regime time
over the evaluation horizon. They are not finite-sample
conditional-probability statements for an individual day (impossible
without distributional assumptions, \citealp{foygelbarber2021limits});
they do not cover the soft-membership or per-stock-offset variants (both
evaluated empirically as ablations); and they do not by themselves
guarantee coverage inside sub-regime events shorter than the convergence
scale. The empirically binding case of the last point is the first days
of a volatility spike (Section~\ref{sec:onset}), where the score
distribution shifts faster than any tracker with bounded step size can
follow.
\end{remark}
