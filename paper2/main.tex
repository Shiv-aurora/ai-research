\title{Conformal Prediction for Realized Volatility across Market Regimes}

\begin{abstract}
Volatility forecast intervals are consumed conditionally, on the bad
days, yet are validated on average. On a point-in-time panel of the 100
largest US stocks (2005--2025; 519{,}843 stock-days), online conformal
intervals for next-day realized volatility are valid on average yet
cover only 83\% of stress-regime days at a 90\% promise --- a shortfall
concentrated in the upper tail and in the fast regime shifts of
2018--2025. We introduce a forecaster-agnostic calibration layer that
pools the calibration signal across stocks, maintains thresholds per
market regime, and sets per-regime learning rates by online expert
aggregation. Pooling supplies most of the average repair; regime
conditioning adds conditional structure and per-regime frequency
guarantees. Stress coverage rises to 88.1\% at no detected cost in
average interval score, and stress-day Value-at-Risk backtests improve
on GARCH-$t$, CAViaR, and conformal baselines. Second-day-of-spike
coverage resists every scheme evaluated; we pose this onset window as an
open problem.
\end{abstract}
