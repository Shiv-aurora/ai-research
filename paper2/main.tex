\begin{abstract}
Volatility forecast intervals are consumed conditionally, on the bad
days, yet are validated on average. On a survivorship-bias-free
point-in-time panel of the 100 largest US stocks (2005--2025; 519{,}843
stock-days), online conformal intervals for next-day realized volatility
are marginally valid but conditionally broken: intervals promised at
90\% cover 83\% of stress-regime days, the shortfall concentrated in
the upper tail. We introduce a
regime-conditional calibration layer wrapping any forecaster: conformal
thresholds are maintained per online-estimated regime, the
calibration signal is pooled across the cross-section, and per-regime
learning rates are set by online expert aggregation, with no tuned parameters. The layer removes most of the conditional deficit (stress
coverage 88.3\%) at no average cost in interval score, delivers the best
stress-day Value-at-Risk backtests, repairs a
foundation model's bands, and carries finite-sample per-regime
guarantees under arbitrary dependence. Day-two-of-spike coverage resists
everything we tried, including oracle regimes; we pose this onset window
as an open problem.
\end{abstract}

\begin{highlights}
\item Marginally valid conformal volatility intervals cover 83\% of
  stress days when 90\% is promised, across 21 years and 200+ stocks.
\item A regime-conditional, cross-sectionally pooled, self-tuning
  conformal layer removes most of the deficit at no average cost.
\item Finite-sample per-regime coverage guarantees hold under arbitrary
  serial and cross-sectional dependence.
\item The layer yields the best stress-day VaR backtests among seven
  methods and repairs a foundation model's miscalibrated bands.
\item Day-2-of-spike coverage resists all conditioning, including oracle
  regimes and option-implied signals: an honest, quantified limit.
\end{highlights}
