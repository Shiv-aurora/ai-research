\begin{abstract}
Volatility forecast intervals are consumed conditionally, on the bad
days, yet are validated on average. On a point-in-time panel of the 100
largest US stocks (2005--2025; 519{,}843 stock-days), online conformal
intervals for next-day realized volatility are marginally valid yet
cover only 83\% of stress-regime days at a 90\% promise, the shortfall
concentrated in the upper tail. We introduce a forecaster-agnostic
calibration layer: the calibration
signal is pooled across the cross-section, thresholds are maintained per
market regime, and per-regime learning rates are set by online expert
aggregation. Pooling supplies most of the average repair; regime
conditioning adds the conditional structure and finite-sample per-regime
frequency guarantees. Stress coverage rises from 82.9\% to 88.1\% with no
detected cost in average interval score, and stress-day Value-at-Risk backtests
improve on GARCH-$t$, CAViaR, and conformal baselines. Second-day-of-spike
coverage resists every scheme we evaluate; we pose this onset window as
an open problem.
\end{abstract}

\begin{highlights}
