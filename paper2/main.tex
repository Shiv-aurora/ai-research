\title{Conformal Prediction for Realized Volatility across Market Regimes}

\begin{abstract}
Volatility forecast intervals are consumed on the bad days yet
validated on average. On a point-in-time panel of the 100
largest US stocks (519{,}843 stock-days, 2005--2025; evaluation
2010--2025; 269{,}705 stock-days in the thirteen-method comparison),
online conformal intervals for next-day realized volatility are valid
on average yet cover only 83\% of stress-regime days at a 90\% promise
--- a shortfall concentrated in the upper tail and in the fast regime
shifts of 2018--2025. A forecaster-agnostic calibration layer pools the
calibration signal across stocks, maintains thresholds per market
regime, and sets its learning rates by online expert
aggregation. Pooling supplies most of the average repair; regime
conditioning adds conditional structure and per-regime frequency
guarantees. Stress coverage rises to 88.1\% with no detected
average interval-score cost, and stress-day Value-at-Risk backtests improve
on GARCH-$t$, CAViaR, and conformal baselines. Second-day-of-spike
coverage resists every scheme evaluated; this onset window remains
open.
\end{abstract}
